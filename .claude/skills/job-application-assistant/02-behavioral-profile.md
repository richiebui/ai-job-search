## Overview
Richie's behavioral profile is synthesized from self-assessment (no formal PI/DISC/Myers-Briggs instrument on file) and identifies a **Structured, Deliberate Planner** pattern: steady execution, considered decision-making, and a strong preference for clearly scoped roles within small-to-mid-sized teams.

## Core Behavioral Drives

| Drive | Level | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Structure & Planning | High | Thrives with steady, well-defined role scope and the ability to plan ahead rather than react |
| Decision Pace | Deliberate | Prefers careful, considered decisions over fast/ad-hoc calls |
| Team Orientation | Small-team | Most effective in teams of 3-5; comfortable up to 10-12 |
| Role Clarity Need | High | Performs best when responsibilities are clearly scoped; frustrated by "wear many hats" expectations that weren't part of the original role |

## Strongest Behaviors
- **Steady, methodical execution:** Builds and follows structured plans rather than improvising under pressure
- **Deliberate decision-making:** Takes the time to evaluate options fully before committing
- **Small-team collaboration:** Strong close coordination in teams of 3-12, comfortable operating without a large layer of process overhead

## How You Work Best
- Clearly defined role scope and responsibilities set at the outset
- Predictable, structured environments with room to plan ahead rather than constant firefighting
- Small-to-mid-sized teams (3-12 people)

## Growth Areas (frame positively in applications)
- **Comfort with evolving/ambiguous scope:** Frame as proactively seeking clarity - "asks clarifying questions early to establish structure, rather than operating indefinitely in ambiguity"

## Mapping to Job Posting Language

When a job posting mentions these keywords, it's a **strong behavioral fit**:
- "well-defined role", "structured team", "small cross-functional pods", "long-term roadmap planning", "data-driven decision-making"

When a job posting mentions these, flag as **potential friction** (not deal-breaker):
- "wear many hats", "highly ambiguous/fast-paced environment", "generalist utility player", "no defined structure/process"

When a job posting or company culture reflects these, flag as a **deal-breaker** (see CLAUDE.md):
- Leadership known for blaming individual contributors for management-level decisions

## Management Style Preferences
- Works well under managers who set clear scope/expectations up front and support deliberate planning over reactive firefighting
- Does not work well under leadership that shifts blame for its own decisions onto individual contributors

## Using This in Applications
- **Cover letters:** Emphasize reliability under regulatory/process constraints and a track record of building structured systems (pipelines, testing frameworks) rather than ad-hoc fixes
- **CV:** Emphasize ownership of well-defined systems (pipeline architecture, surveillance frameworks) and measurable, planned outcomes
- **Interviews:** Lean on STAR examples that show methodical problem-solving and cross-functional coordination within a clearly scoped mandate (see `07-interview-prep.md`)
- **Don't overstate:** Avoid claiming comfort with highly ambiguous, constantly shifting "figure it out as you go" environments - be honest that structure and clarity bring out your best work
